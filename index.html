The problem

Static routes leave money behind.

01

Approval rates move constantly

Approval performance changes by provider, market, issuer, payment method, and time. A fixed route cannot follow those changes.

02

Providers degrade silently

A working PSP can start failing before a payment team notices. Every minute of manual reaction time is failed deposit volume.

03

Declines are not always final

A declined deposit may still be eligible for another approved route. Without controlled cascading, that volume is simply lost.

The route that performed best yesterday may not be the best route for the next deposit. Smart Cashier is designed to make the decision using current performance, transaction context, and operator-defined rules.

How it works

One decision layer across your payment stack.

Step 1

Receive the deposit request

Smart Cashier receives the deposit context through the API, SDK, or cashier flow.

Step 2

Respond and learn

Route around provider outages

Health checks and circuit breakers watch availability, error rates, and latency. When a route degrades past a threshold, eligible traffic shifts to an alternative provider instead of waiting for a person to notice the outage.

FAQ

What operators need to know.

What is Smart Cashier?

Smart Cashier is payment orchestration software for iGaming operators. It sits between your cashier and your payment service providers (PSPs) and routes each eligible deposit to the route with the strongest expected outcome, based on recent performance, availability, cost, and your rules.

What is payment orchestration?

Payment orchestration is a control layer that manages how transactions are distributed across multiple PSPs, gateways, and payment methods. Instead of one static route, the orchestration layer selects the best available route for each transaction and moves traffic when conditions change.

Is Smart Cashier a payment service provider?

No. Smart Cashier is orchestration software, not a regulated payment provider. It does not process, acquire, or settle funds itself. Money continues to move through your existing PSPs and acquirers.

Does Smart Cashier replace our existing PSPs?

No. It works on top of the PSPs you already have contracts with, and helps you get more value from that mix. You keep your provider relationships and can add or remove providers behind one integration layer.

Is the product already processing live customer payments?

The current public version is a working product demo. Production PSP integrations and routing scope are implemented around each operator's existing cashier and PSP stack as part of the commercial engagement.

What does the public demo show?

The demo shows the deposit experience from the player's point of view: amount selection, route evaluation, payment methods compared by approval-style metrics, a recommended route, and a confirmation. It illustrates the product concept and interface.

Are the demo metrics real customer results?

No. All numbers in the demo (approval rates, deposit counts, provider counts, routing times) are demonstration data used to illustrate the interface. They are not customer results.

What information can a routing decision use?

The routing model can use recent approval rates, country, BIN, card issuer, bank, currency, deposit amount, payment method, device, time, provider availability, gateway load, processing cost, and operator-defined priorities. The exact signals depend on the agreed integration scope and applicable law.

What happens when a PSP becomes unavailable?

Smart Cashier is designed to monitor availability, error rates, latency, and timeouts. When a route crosses an operator-defined threshold, eligible traffic can be moved to an alternative approved route automatically, so an outage does not stop the entire deposit flow.

Does Smart Cashier support cascading?

Yes, in a controlled form. For eligible soft declines, a deposit can be retried through another approved route according to operator-defined rules: decline-code eligibility, retry limits, time windows, and provider restrictions. Not every declined payment should be retried.

How do you prevent duplicate charges during retries?

Cascading is designed with duplicate-charge protection: idempotent retry logic, decline-code eligibility checks, retry limits, time windows, and a full retry history for every transaction.

Does Smart Cashier support deposits and withdrawals?

The public offer focuses on deposits. Withdrawal orchestration is not part of the current public offer.
