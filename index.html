The problem

Static routes leave money behind.

A failed deposit is money a player tried to give you. Most of it is lost for reasons a fixed routing setup cannot see.

01

Approval rates move constantly

Performance changes by provider, market, issuer, method, and time of day. A fixed route cannot follow those changes.

02

Providers degrade silently

A PSP can start failing before anyone notices. Every minute of manual reaction time is failed deposit volume.

03

Declines are not always final

Many declined deposits would be approved by a different provider. Without controlled retries, that volume is simply lost.

Built around your payment data

Your routing problem is specific to your PSP mix.

Approval performance varies by provider, market, bank, BIN, payment method, amount, time, and player segment. Smart Cashier is configured around your historical payment data, existing integrations, decline patterns, commercial priorities, and infrastructure requirements.

The goal is not to apply one generic routing model to every operator. The goal is to identify where your deposits are being lost, test better routing decisions, and deploy the logic that works for your operation.

How it works

One decision layer across your payment stack.

In plain terms: for every deposit, Smart Cashier picks the payment route that is most likely to succeed right now, and explains why.

Step 1

Receive the deposit

The deposit context arrives through the API, SDK, or cashier flow.

Step 2

Compare the routes

Availability, approval performance for the last 60 minutes, transaction context, cost, and your rules.

Step 3

Route the deposit

The deposit goes to the strongest eligible route. If a provider degrades, traffic shifts automatically.

Step 4

Record and learn

Every outcome and every decision reason is stored for analytics and audit.

Controlled validation

Validate the routing model before a full deployment.

You are not asked to replace your payment infrastructure. The pilot measures the opportunity using your payment data while limiting operational risk. The exact scope is agreed around your cashier, PSPs, markets, and technical constraints.

Stage 1

Payment data assessment

We review anonymized or appropriately protected historical deposit data to find approval gaps, PSP differences, decline patterns, outages, and segments where another route may perform better.

potential inputs:
attempts · outcomes · PSP · method · country · BIN · issuer · amount · currency · decline code · processing time · existing route
Stage 2

Historical replay

The routing model can be tested against historical transactions to compare your existing routing decisions with alternative route-selection logic.

Historical replay identifies potential opportunity. It does not guarantee the same result in live traffic.

compares:
your routes vs model routes, segment by segment
Stage 3

Shadow routing

The pilot scope can include shadow mode: Smart Cashier generates routing recommendations alongside your existing payment flow without controlling live transactions, so both decision models can be compared under current conditions.

risk to live traffic:
none in shadow mode
Stage 4

Controlled traffic

Where appropriate, a limited share of eligible deposits can be routed through the new logic, using agreed markets, PSPs, traffic limits, fallback rules, and success criteria.

possible controls:
one country · one brand · 2-3 PSPs · traffic caps · retry limits · manual override · automatic rollback · agreed monitoring
Stage 5

Source-code deployment

After validation, the routing layer can be deployed on your infrastructure under a perpetual non-exclusive source-code license.

you receive:
full source code · docs · deployment scripts · training · support

FAQ

What operators ask first.

How is Smart Cashier tailored to our payment stack?

The routing scope is defined around your cashier, PSPs, payment methods, markets, historical approval data, decline patterns, routing rules, commercial priorities, and infrastructure requirements.

What data is needed for a pilot?

The exact data set depends on the pilot scope. It may include deposit attempts, approval outcomes, PSP, market, payment method, amount, currency, BIN or issuer segment, decline code, processing time, and existing route. Data access and protection requirements are agreed before analysis begins.

Does the pilot affect live payment traffic?

Not necessarily. A pilot can begin with historical analysis or shadow routing. Limited live traffic is used only when appropriate and agreed with the operator.

How do you measure whether routing is improving?

Success criteria are defined before implementation. They may include approval-rate improvement, recovered eligible deposits, reduced outage exposure, routing accuracy, lower timeout rates, or better performance in selected segments.

What do we receive with the source-code license?

Full source-code access under a perpetual non-exclusive license, internal-use and modification rights, on-premise or private-cloud deployment, documentation, deployment scripts, integration support, architecture review, team training, updates under the agreement, and priority support.

Can Smart Cashier run without vendor access to payment data?

Yes. Self-hosted deployment is designed so the software can run entirely on your infrastructure, without vendor access to payment data.

Which PSPs can be integrated?

Integration scope is defined around the PSPs, gateways, payment methods, and APIs in the operator's existing stack. The website does not publish an unsupported universal integration list.

Does Smart Cashier replace our PSPs?

No. It routes deposits across the PSPs you already have contracts with. You keep your provider relationships. Smart Cashier is orchestration software, not a payment provider.

What happens after we submit a request?

We reply by email to arrange a short discovery call and a technical review of your payment stack. There is no obligation attached to submitting the form.
