Payment orchestration for sportsbooks.

Sportsbook deposits concentrate around events. A provider outage ten minutes before a major match is not an infrastructure problem, it is lost deposit volume at the worst possible moment. Smart Cashier gives your payment stack one decision layer that reacts in real time.

Peak load is where static routing breaks.

Deposit traffic in betting is spiky by nature: match days, tournaments, and live events compress a large share of monthly volume into short windows. That is exactly when providers are most likely to slow down, time out, or throttle.

If the route is fixed, every failed attempt during the peak is a player who may not try again before the odds change.

How Smart Cashier addresses it.

The routing layer monitors availability, error rates, latency, and timeout frequency per provider. When a route crosses an operator-defined threshold, eligible traffic moves to an alternative approved route automatically, so an outage does not stop the deposit flow.

Outside of incidents, performance-based routing sends each deposit to the provider with the strongest recent results for that segment: market, method, issuer, amount. Rules, caps, A/B tests, and manual override stay in your team's hands.

What this can look like in numbers.

Illustrative scenario
Monthly deposit attempts: $6,000,000
Attempted volume per hour: ≈ $8,333
Three-hour route outage exposure: ≈ $25,000
If 80% is redirected: ≈ $20,000 stays in flow

Illustrative scenario. Additional approved deposit volume is not revenue or profit, and no uplift is guaranteed. Results depend on your traffic, PSP mix, markets, decline reasons, and rules.

Common questions.

How fast does failover react?

Failover reacts to operator-defined thresholds on live health signals, so traffic can shift as soon as a route crosses the threshold rather than after manual escalation.

Can we cap traffic per provider?

Yes. Rules include traffic allocation, caps, priorities, and fallback order.

Do you support both casino and sportsbook brands?

Yes. Routing logic can differ per brand, market, and campaign under one integration layer.
