Self-hosted payment orchestration for iGaming

Payment orchestration for iGaming, explained.

Payment orchestration is a control layer between your cashier and your payment service providers (PSPs). Instead of one static route, it selects the best available route for each deposit and moves traffic when conditions change. Here is what that means in practice.

View the demo

The building blocks.

Performance-based routing sends each eligible deposit to the provider with the strongest recent approval rate for that segment. Live health monitoring watches availability, error rates, latency, and timeouts. Automatic failover moves traffic when a route degrades. Controlled cascading retries eligible soft declines through another approved route.

Around the routing engine sit the operational tools: rules, traffic allocation, A/B testing, manual override, audit logs, and analytics that make payment losses measurable instead of hidden inside aggregate decline data.

What orchestration is not.

An orchestration layer is not a PSP, an acquirer, or a payment institution, and it does not replace KYC, AML, fraud platforms, or the operator's licensing responsibilities. Money continues to move through your existing providers.

Smart Cashier applies this model specifically to iGaming deposits, with a pilot-first commercial path: validate the routing model on your own payment data, then deploy the full source code on your infrastructure.

Illustrative scenario. Additional approved deposit volume is not revenue or profit, and no uplift is guaranteed. Results depend on your traffic, PSP mix, markets, decline reasons, and rules.

Common questions.

Is payment orchestration only for large operators?

No. The best fit is any operator with multiple PSPs and meaningful failed deposit volume, typically from around $100,000 in failed deposits per month.

Does orchestration require replacing our cashier?

No. The routing engine integrates through an API and SDK. An optional cashier UI is available if you want a ready deposit interface.

What is the first step?

A discovery call and technical review of your payment stack, followed by a controlled pilot with agreed success criteria.
