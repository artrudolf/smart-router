Self-hosted payment orchestration for iGaming

iGaming PSP cascading, done with control.

A declined deposit is not always a lost deposit. Many soft declines are route-specific: the same payment may be approved by a different provider. Cascading retries eligible declines through another approved route, under rules your team defines.

View the demo

Why naive retries are dangerous.

Blindly retrying every decline creates duplicate-charge risk, issuer flags, and unhappy players. Some decline codes should never be retried. Cascading only creates value when it is controlled.

Smart Cashier's cascading is built around eligibility: decline-code rules decide what may be retried, retry limits and time windows decide how often, and provider, country, and payment-method restrictions decide where.

What controlled cascading includes.

Decline-code eligibility, retry limits, duplicate-charge protection, time windows, provider restrictions, country and payment-method rules, player-risk rules where applicable, and a full retry history for every transaction.

The result: deposits that would end at the first decline can be recovered, without retry storms and without retrying payments that should stay declined.

Common questions.

Should every declined payment be retried?

No. Only declines that are eligible under decline-code rules should be retried. Hard declines and risk-related declines are excluded.

How are duplicate charges prevented?

Retries use idempotent logic with duplicate-charge protection, retry limits, and time windows, and every retry is recorded in the transaction history.

Can we see why a retry happened?

Yes. Route-selection reasons and the full retry history are available in analytics and audit logs.
